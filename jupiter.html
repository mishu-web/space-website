Jupiter: The Gas Giant

The largest planet in our solar system and a mini solar system of its own

Introduction to Jupiter

Jupiter, the fifth planet from the Sun, is a massive gas giant that dominates our solar system. With its iconic Great Red Spot and numerous moons, Jupiter is a fascinating world that has been studied for centuries. It's more than twice as massive as all other planets combined and plays a crucial role in protecting Earth by deflecting comets and asteroids.

Quick Jupiter Facts

Diameter: 139,820 km (11x Earth's diameter)
Mass: 318 times Earth's mass
Surface Gravity: 2.4 times Earth's
Average Temperature: -145°C (-234°F)
Day Length: 9 hours, 56 minutes
Year Length: 11.86 Earth years

Atmospheric Features

Jupiter's atmosphere is a turbulent mix of colorful clouds and violent storms:

  • Great Red Spot: A giant storm larger than Earth that has raged for centuries
  • Cloud Bands: Alternating light and dark bands caused by powerful jet streams
  • Polar Cyclones: Massive cyclones at both poles, each larger than the United States
  • Lightning Storms: Powerful electrical discharges deeper in the atmosphere

Moons of Jupiter

Jupiter has at least 79 known moons, forming its own miniature solar system:

  • Galilean Moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto discovered by Galileo
  • Europa: Ice-covered moon with a subsurface ocean that may harbor life
  • Io: Most volcanically active body in the solar system
  • Ganymede: Largest moon in the solar system (bigger than Mercury)

Exploration

Several spacecraft have studied Jupiter up close:

  • Pioneer 10 & 11: First to fly by Jupiter (1973-1974)
  • Voyager 1 & 2: Revealed volcanic activity on Io (1979)
  • Galileo: First orbiter (1995-2003)
  • Juno: Currently studying Jupiter's composition and magnetic field

Did You Know?

Jupiter's magnetic field is the strongest in the solar system (after the Sun), about 20,000 times stronger than Earth's. This creates spectacular auroras at its poles that are hundreds of times more powerful than Earth's northern lights!
