The Moon: Earth's Faithful Satellite

The near side of the Moon as seen from Earth (NASA)

Lunar Quick Facts

Distance from Earth: 384,400 km (avg)
Orbital Period: 27.3 days (sidereal)
Rotation Period: 27.3 days (tidally locked)
Diameter: 3,474 km (1/4 Earth's)
Mass: 1.2% Earth's mass
Surface Gravity: 16.5% Earth's
Surface Temp Range: -173°C to 127°C (-280°F to 260°F)

1. Lunar Formation & Evolution

A. Giant Impact Hypothesis

Artist's concept of the giant impact that may have formed the Moon (NASA/JPL-Caltech)

The leading theory suggests:

  • Mars-sized body (Theia) collided with early Earth ~4.5 billion years ago
  • Ejected material formed a debris ring
  • Accreted into the Moon within ~100 years

B. Geological History

  • Magma Ocean: Initial molten surface (first 200 million years)
  • Heavy Bombardment: Intense meteor impacts until ~3.8 billion years ago
  • Volcanic Activity: Lava flows created maria (dark plains) until ~1 billion years ago

Size Matters Fact: The Moon is proportionally the largest satellite in the solar system compared to its planet - 1/4 Earth's diameter (other moons are typically 1/100 their planet's size)!

2. Lunar Features

Surface Composition

Geological features of the Moon's surface (NASA)

Highlands: Light-colored, ancient crust (anorthosite)
Maria: Dark volcanic plains (basalt)
Regolith: Fine dusty soil covering entire surface (4-15m deep)

A. Impact Craters

Close-up of lunar craters showing different sizes and features (NASA)

  • Tycho: Young, prominent ray system (108 km diameter)
  • Copernicus: Well-preserved complex crater (93 km)
  • South Pole-Aitken Basin: Largest impact feature (2,500 km diameter)

B. Mountains & Valleys

  • Apennine Mountains: Highest peaks (5 km)
  • Vallis Alpes: Stunning alpine valley (180 km long)
  • Rilles: Collapsed lava tubes (Hadley Rille visited by Apollo 15)

3. Moon's Influence on Earth

A. Tidal Effects

Diagram showing the Moon's effect on Earth's tides (NASA)

  • Ocean tides (with Sun's contribution)
  • Earth's rotation slowing (~2.3 ms/century)
  • Stabilization of Earth's axial tilt (prevents extreme climate shifts)

B. Cultural Impact

  • Calendar systems (lunar months)
  • Mythology and folklore worldwide
  • Inspiration for art and literature

Drifting Away Fact: The Moon is receding from Earth at 3.8 cm/year - in the dinosaur era (100 million years ago), a day was only 23 hours long and the Moon appeared 15% larger!

4. Lunar Exploration

A. Major Missions

Apollo astronaut on the lunar surface (NASA)

  • Apollo Program (1969-72): 6 manned landings, 12 astronauts walked on surface
  • Luna Program (USSR): First spacecraft to reach Moon (1959), first robotic sample return
  • Chang'e Program (China): Recent landings including far side (Chang'e 4)

B. Scientific Discoveries

  • Moon rocks revealed similar oxygen isotopes to Earth
  • Confirmed lack of atmosphere and magnetic field
  • Evidence of water ice in permanently shadowed craters

5. Future of Lunar Exploration

Upcoming Missions

Concept art of Artemis mission to the Moon (NASA)

Artemis Program (NASA): Return humans to Moon by 2026, establish sustainable presence
Lunar Gateway: Planned space station in lunar orbit
International Bases: Planned by US, China, Europe, and private companies

A. Scientific Goals

  • Study lunar interior structure
  • Test technologies for Mars missions
  • Search for resources (water, helium-3)
