JAMES WEBB SPACE TELESCOPE

The most powerful space telescope ever built is revolutionizing our understanding of the universe.

Launched in December 2021, JWST can look further back in time than any previous telescope, observing the first galaxies that formed after the Big Bang. Its infrared capabilities allow it to peer through cosmic dust clouds where stars and planetary systems are being born.

  • Primary mirror: 6.5 meters (21.3 feet) in diameter
  • Operating temperature: -223°C (-370°F)
  • Orbit location: 1.5 million km from Earth

OUR PALE BLUE DOT

Earth as seen from space reminds us of our planet's fragility and beauty.

This perspective, first captured by Voyager 1 from 6 billion kilometers away, shows Earth as a mere speck in the vast cosmic arena. Our atmosphere, thinner relative to Earth's size than the skin of an apple, protects all known life from the harshness of space.

EARTH FACTS
  • Age: 4.54 billion years
  • Surface area: 510 million km²
  • 71% covered by water
ORBIT DETAILS
  • Orbital speed: 107,000 km/h
  • Distance from Sun: 150 million km
  • Axial tilt: 23.5 degrees

OUR GALACTIC HOME

The Milky Way galaxy is our cosmic neighborhood, containing over 100 billion stars.

Our solar system resides in one of the Milky Way's spiral arms, about 27,000 light-years from the galactic center. It takes our Sun about 225-250 million years to complete one orbit around the galaxy's center - a period called a cosmic year.
